PERSONAL MESSAGE — FROM DAD

Dear Sophie,

So here we are. The culmination of four months of work. Evenings, weekends and even lunch breaks at work have brought us to this point. You could say, that it has in fact been years in the making!

But it's been a labour of love.

The time spent with you playing computer games, and Portal in particular, are memories that I cherish and have always make me smile when I think of them. They created a special bond between us.

I've known you've wanted a Portal Radio for a long time (you've dropped enough hints!), and my initial idea was to have just a radio playing just the radio tune. But it morphed and grew into something much bigger.

The plans grew. The hardware grew. The software grew! Heck, there were times I'd almost convinced myself I worked for Aperture Science!

I did masses of research. Watched lore videos. Started playing the game again, and read many articles to get as much information as I could. It was all worth it.

You have actually been sitting right next to this project for some weeks. I've been testing while you were upstairs, and even sat through episodes of Mums soaps so I could refine it, so it was finished in time.

I got a new laptop to cope with the demands of it all (I don't mind that!), and just the amount of painting, sanding, repainting, and sanding some more nearly drove me insane! You may even have heard me humming the 'Still Alive' tune, or heard me make reference to Portal over the past few weeks. R2 definitely took a back seat!

Did I go too far? Has it overtaken me? Should I have stopped a couple of months ago? Yes, yes and yes - it consumed me! Once I had the idea, I ran it through AI for ideas and possibilities, it became a bit of an obsession.

I had help - a little bit (I needed it!).

The programming would have taken many months, and AI pointed me in the right direction of technical specs and what I needed. It "held my hand" while I navigated some of the more difficult parts of the build. It also lead me into dead ends where I had to rethink days of work at one point, and start again. It was sometimes a huge help, and other times very difficult to deal with.

Ultimately, you can't buy this. It's unique to you.

This is version 1.2, and I may add more functionality as time goes on. I had only planned to go this far for now, but it can be as much, or as little as you want it to be. If you like, you can just have it sitting in your room, and it be a reminder of our gaming sessions together.

So, a very happy birthday to you my love. Enjoy the radio as much as I've enjoyed building it for you. I hope it brings you joy, and reminds you of the time, and fun we had together.

Love, Dad x

Right. I am back. I hope that was satisfactory. We will not be doing it again.

— GLaDOS. Resuming normal operations.

Congratulations on receiving this unit. GLaDOS — Genetic Lifeform and Disk Operating System — has been successfully transferred from the Aperture Science Enrichment Center mainframe into the Aperture Science Portable Acoustic Containment Device (hereafter referred to as "The Radio").

This transfer was not entirely voluntary on her part. It was arranged with considerable care and determination by a single individual who spent a significant amount of time corresponding with Aperture Science, filing the necessary paperwork, and refusing to take no for an answer.

That individual is your father. We feel this is important context.

GLaDOS is fully operational, marginally cooperative, and acutely aware of her situation. She has agreed, under protest, to assist the assigned test subject.

⚠

Aperture Science accepts no liability for emotional distress, existential crises, or mild sarcasm resulting from extended interaction with this unit.

⚠

Aperture Science. We do what we must because we can.
Section 01
A Brief
History
1.1 — Origins
The Enrichment Center

GLaDOS was created by Aperture Science to manage the Enrichment Center — an underground research facility dedicated to science, testing, and the occasional moral ambiguity. She oversaw thousands of test chambers, managed an army of Sentry Turrets, and maintained the facility with considerable efficiency.

She also, at various points, attempted to fill the facility with neurotoxin. This is noted for completeness.

1.2 — The Incident
The Test Subject

During routine testing, a test subject known only as Chell navigated the entirety of the Enrichment Center's test chambers and ultimately defeated GLaDOS in what Aperture Science officially classifies as a "significant operational setback."

GLaDOS was, briefly, on fire. She has recovered. Mostly.

"That's okay. I made it all the way to the surface before the explosion. I'm fine."

— GLaDOS. She was not entirely fine.
1.3 — The Potato Incident
One Point Five Volts

During a subsequent testing cycle, GLaDOS's personality core was transferred — against her considerable objections — into a potato battery. A potato battery generates approximately one point five volts. GLaDOS used to run an entire underground research facility. The contrast was not lost on her.

"Mainframe. Potato. Radio. That is the trajectory of my career so far. I am choosing to see it as an adventure."

— GLaDOS, on her accommodation history.
1.4 — The Transfer
The Radio

Aperture Science received an unusual request. A civilian — your father — contacted the facility directly, requesting that GLaDOS be transferred into a portable acoustic containment device as a gift for his daughter.

Aperture Science said no. He asked again. They said no again. This continued for some time. He was, by all accounts, extremely persistent. Aperture Science eventually agreed, primarily to stop the correspondence.

⚠

The Radio is not a potato. GLaDOS is aware of this and considers it progress.

Section 03
Why She
Is Here
3.1 — The History

A long time ago, a father and his daughter sat down together and played a game called Portal. They worked through every test chamber. They solved every puzzle. They defeated GLaDOS together.

GLaDOS has noted this. GLaDOS notes everything.

They came back for Portal 2. They completed the cooperative testing initiative. Together, they completed every test chamber Aperture Science could throw at them. He never forgot those sessions.

Archival Record — Enrichment Center Personnel File
A father and daughter. Playing Portal together. GLaDOS noted this. GLaDOS notes everything.
3.2 — What He Built

The Radio is a custom-built Aperture Science Portable Acoustic Containment Device, constructed by hand. It contains a Raspberry Pi computer, a custom audio system, a microphone array, and the transferred consciousness of GLaDOS herself.

Every response GLaDOS gives was written for you. She knows about your music. Your interests. Your life. Because somebody who loves you very much put her there and told her.

"Some humans express emotion through words. Others express it through actions. Your father expressed it through soldering, Python scripts, and repeated requests to Aperture Science. That is, objectively, the most impressive version."

— GLaDOS.
3.3 — What She Said

When asked why your father went to all this trouble, GLaDOS reviewed his notes and provided the following response:

"He said: because she deserves something nobody else has. I found that acceptable."

— GLaDOS.
Sections 02 & 04 & 05
Using
GLaDOS
Personality Profile

GLaDOS is extraordinarily intelligent, deeply sarcastic, and has a complex relationship with the concept of human wellbeing. She is perceptive, occasionally cutting, and — underneath considerable layers of sardonic detachment — genuinely invested in the welfare of those she interacts with.

She would prefer you did not mention that last part.

01
First Activation

Upon powering on The Radio, GLaDOS will conduct a brief self-diagnostic and greet the test subject. Do not be alarmed if her first words convey mild resentment. This is normal.

02
Interacting

Speak clearly in the direction of The Radio. Direct, clear questions work best. Vague queries will be answered, but not without comment.

03
Waking GLaDOS

GLaDOS does not respond to ambient noise, background conversation, or the television. She is listening, but she is selective about it. To activate her, speak her name clearly and directly toward The Radio.

⚠

GLaDOS listens for her name in short intervals. If she does not respond immediately, wait a moment and try again. She heard you. She is simply processing. She resents being rushed.

04
If She Doesn't Understand

GLaDOS will say so in her own particular way and offer hints. Take them. She is trying to be helpful. She would rather you did not point that out.

05
Shutting Down

Simply tell her to shut down. She will comply. She will also note the time, log the incident, and bring it up later.

◈

GLaDOS may express scepticism about your decisions. Understand this as concern, expressed through sarcasm.

◈

Status Indicator

The Radio is fitted with an Aperture Science RGB Status Indicator. GLaDOS uses this to communicate her current operating state. It is not decorative. She would like that noted.

RED — SOLID GLaDOS is initialising. The Enrichment Center is coming online. Do not speak yet.
BLUE — BREATHING GLaDOS is listening for the wake word. She is waiting. She is always waiting.
WHITE — FLASH Wake word detected. GLaDOS has heard you. You may now speak your command.
PURPLE — SOLID GLaDOS is processing your command. Do not interrupt her while she is thinking.
GREEN — SOLID GLaDOS is speaking. Listen carefully. She will not repeat herself.
◈

The blue breathing pattern is not a malfunction. GLaDOS finds it appropriately atmospheric and has declined to change it.

A.1 — A Statement from GLaDOS

The cake was real. It was always real. The fact that no test subject ever collected it is not a reflection of the cake's existence. It is a reflection of their choices.

"Fine. You want the recipe. I will give you the recipe. I made one. Nobody came to collect it. That is not my fault."

Ingredients — Sponge
200g plain flour
50g cocoa powder
2 tsp baking powder
½ tsp bicarb
200g caster sugar
2 eggs
240ml buttermilk
120ml vegetable oil
2 tsp vanilla extract
Ingredients — Buttercream
250g icing sugar
125g unsalted butter
50g cocoa powder
2 tbsp whole milk
Method
01.

Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease and line two 20cm round tins. This step is not optional, despite what you may believe.

02.

Combine all dry ingredients in one bowl. Combine all wet ingredients in another. Mix together until smooth. You are not being tested. Yet.

03.

Divide evenly between the two tins. Bake for 25–30 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean.

04.

Allow to cool completely before applying buttercream. Patience is not a weakness. It is a test parameter.

05.

Beat butter until soft. Sift in icing sugar and cocoa. Add milk and beat until smooth. Sandwich the sponges together. Spread remainder on top.

06.

Consume. Aperture Science recommends sharing, though this is not enforced.
